ZenMind

Role: UX Designer & Researcher
Duration: 10 months

Zenmind is an app designed to support individuals managing anxiety and panic disorders. The objective was to create a calming, user-friendly tool that offers guided support, exercises, and coping strategies. My role covered end-to-end UX design and user research—from initial discovery to final handoff.

My Process

Discovery & Research

I began with competitive analysis, and in-depth user research, including one-on-one interviews with individuals managing anxiety. This helped surface key pain points, emotional triggers, and gaps in existing mental health apps.

Define & Ideate

Based on research insights, I defined user personas and journey maps. This led to early sketches and wireframes for core functionalities like breathing exercises, journaling feature, and emergency support.

Design & Prototype

I created mid- and high-fidelity prototypes in Figma, focusing on a minimal, soothing visual language. Accessibility and emotional sensitivity were prioritized in UI choices, from color palette to copy. The prototypes were tested in usability sessions and refined through multiple iterations.

Testing & Handoff

Usability testing revealed opportunities to improve navigation clarity and reduce friction in onboarding.

Conclusion

Zenmind was a deeply rewarding project that challenged me to balance functionality with emotional awareness. The final product reflected not just user needs but also their emotional states, providing accessible, human-centered support during vulnerable moments. This project strengthened my skills in research-led design, accessibility, and working cross-functionally with dev teams.
